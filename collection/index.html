2667 is more than a number—it's the edition of the Bored Ape that became one of the defining pieces in my journey as a collector. I started collecting NFTs to connect with groundbreaking digital artists and be part of a cultural shift in how we create, share, and value art. My collection reflects that journey—featuring work from visionaries like Pak and Beeple alongside a growing list of other creators I believe in. For me, it's never been about flipping or chasing the next big drop; it's about building a collection that tells a story and supports the artists shaping the future of digital creativity.

The Title

by Pak

Released in December 2020, The Title is a conceptual NFT collection built around a single repeated image sold at different prices, with only the token's name changing. Each NFT shares the same artwork, but variations in title and scarcity highlight how value in crypto art often stems more from ownership, labeling, and perceived rarity than from visual difference. One work in the series, pointedly named "The Unsold," carried a $1,000,000 list price, sharpening the project's critique of speculation and value in the NFT space.

Ash Chapter One: Carbon

by Pak

Released in January 2022 as the first drop in Pak's ASH project, Ash Chapter One: Carbon includes one unique 1-of-1 NFT and 999 editioned NFTs. It uses a burn system where users burn ASH tokens to create, upgrade, or delete the artworks. This setup makes NFTs part of a changing system of value, where burned items leave behind ash that acts as both money and a symbol of how value changes in digital art.

Ash Chapter Two: Metamorphosis

by Pak (featuring 30 artists)

Released in March 2022 as the second drop in Pak's ASH project, Ash Chapter Two: Metamorphosis offers an edition of 7500 tokens. Each wallet can mint up to 10 tokens revealing art from 30 creators like Pak, Paris Hilton, and Mad Dog Jones (250 per creator), with mechanics to combine any 5 tokens to morph one—destroying 4 to reduce supply and enable high-tier burns. This setup turns initial artworks into evolving forms, probing how scarcity, destruction, and adaptation redefine value in digital art.
