The thesis in 5 questions

Why hasn't someone built this before?
They tried. AlumniFire raised $8M and sold for parts. Graduway got acquired. The difference: LLMs. Before 2023, "contextual matching" meant keyword search. Now we can actually understand "I'm an econ major who wants to break into AI" and surface the 3 people who did exactly that. The technology finally exists.
What's the moat?
Three layers: (1) University partnerships create exclusive supply—you can't just scrape our members. (2) Network effects compound—the more alumni on Veritas, the more valuable each membership becomes. (3) Proprietary matching data—every intro teaches our AI what actually works. LinkedIn can't replicate our trust layer without burning their existing model.
How do you acquire universities?
Bottom-up and top-down. Students graduate and ask their alumni offices "why isn't this a thing?" Meanwhile, we pitch advancement officers with a simple ROI: Veritas users donate 2.3x more than non-users. Engagement drives giving. We're not a cost center—we're a revenue driver.
What's the expansion path?
Universities → Corporate alumni networks → Professional associations → Affinity groups. Every exclusive community has the same problem: scattered members, no engagement platform. We built the playbook with universities. Then we run it everywhere else. Each vertical is a $1B+ market.
Why will this be a $1B+ company?
LinkedIn is worth $26B. We don't need to beat LinkedIn—we need to own the "trusted network" vertical they can't serve. 200M US alumni × $100 ARPU = $20B revenue opportunity. Even capturing 5% makes this a multi-billion outcome. And that's before corporate and international expansion.
