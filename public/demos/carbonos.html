The hard questions, answered.

Market & Timing

Why now?

Three regulatory forcing functions hit simultaneously: EU CSRD (2024), SEC climate rules (2026), and California SB 253 (2027). 50,000+ companies suddenly need carbon accounting. They can't all hire consultants.

How big is the market?

Carbon accounting software is $12B by 2030 (Verdantix). But that understates it—every company eventually needs this. CSRD alone creates $2B+ in new compliance spend.

What about Persefoni, Watershed?

They're selling to Fortune 500 with 12-month enterprise sales cycles. We're Stripe to their Oracle—self-serve, API-first, transparent pricing. Different customer, different motion.

Can incumbents just add this?

SAP and Salesforce have tried. But carbon accounting requires specialized emission factors, supply chain data, and regulatory expertise. It's not a feature—it's a platform. Their bolt-ons don't work.

Product & Moat

What's the moat?

Network effects. Every company on CarbonOS shares supplier data. 52,000 suppliers reporting means instant Scope 3 for new customers. More customers → better data → more customers.

How accurate is the data?

Industry-best. We use 1.2M emission factors from EPA, DEFRA, ecoinvent. Primary data from suppliers beats estimates. Third-party verified methodology. Auditors love us.

How do you get Scope 3 data?

Three ways: (1) Direct supplier data through our network, (2) spend-based estimates using industry benchmarks, (3) product-level LCA data. We show confidence scores so you know what's measured vs. estimated.

What's the expansion path?

Start with compliance → add reduction tools → sell carbon credits → become the operating system for corporate climate. $499/mo grows to $50K+ as customers mature.
